## Finding 1 — AraS2P (arXiv:2509.23504, ArabicNLP 2025): #1 IqraEval 2025 — Best Speech-to-Phonemes System for Tajweed Detection

**What it is:** AraS2P ("Arabic Speech-to-Phonemes") is a Wav2Vec2-BERT system submitted to the IqraEval 2025 Shared Task (ArabicNLP @ EMNLP 2025). It **ranked #1 on the official IqraEval 2025 leaderboard** for phoneme-level mispronunciation detection in Arabic recitation.

**Architecture:**
- Base: `Wav2Vec2-BERT` (cross-lingual, strong on Arabic phonemes)
- Stage 1: Task-adaptive continued pretraining on large-scale Arabic speech-phonemes datasets generated by converting Arabic text with the MSA Phonetiser (massive scale, weak labels)
- Stage 2: Fine-tuned on IqraEval 2025 official shared task data + augmentation with XTTS-v2 synthesized recitations featuring varied Ayah segments, speaker embeddings, and textual perturbations to simulate human errors

**Why it matters for Sakīna:**
This is the direct phoneme detection backbone Sakīna needs for its tajweed feedback layer. Ranked above all other teams at IqraEval 2025 on phoneme-level mispronunciation detection. The augmentation strategy (XTTS-v2 synthetic recitations with deliberate errors) is exactly what Sakīna needs to train on diverse tajweed violation patterns without collecting massive annotated human error data.

**arXiv:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.23504  
**ACL Anthology:** https://aclanthology.org/2025.arabicnlp-sharedtasks.65.pdf  
**License:** Research — check paper for details  
**HuggingFace model:** Not yet found in public model zoo; check if released post-paper

**Sakīna action:** Compare AraS2P against the Round 12 IQRA 2026 winner (wav2vec2-xls-r-300m + TCN + CTC, F1=0.7201). AraS2P won IqraEval 2025 (QuranMB.v1), while whu-iasp won IQRA 2026 (QuranMB.v2). The IQRA 2026 winner (F1=0.7201) is the more recent and likely stronger system. Use whu-iasp architecture as primary template; request AraS2P weights from authors for comparison.

## Finding 2 — TBOGamer22/wav2vec2-quran-phonetics (Dec 9, 2025): First Public wav2vec2 Model for Quranic Phonetic Transcription

**What it is:** `TBOGamer22/wav2vec2-quran-phonetics` — uploaded December 9, 2025 to HuggingFace. Described as "the first publicly released wav2vec2 model trained explicitly for Quranic phonetic transcription."

**Architecture:**
- Base: wav2vec2 with **unfrozen feature extractor** (allows acoustic adaptation to Quranic recitation — unlike typical ASR fine-tunes where the feature extractor is frozen)
- Training data: word-level Quranic recitation audio with aligned metadata
- Output: phonetic (sound-level) representations suitable for tajweed research and education

**Key difference from other Quran ASR models:**
Every other public Quran ASR model (KheemP/whisper-base-quran-lora, tarteel-ai/whisper-base-ar-quran, Moonshine Arabic) outputs Arabic text characters. This model outputs phonetic representations — the layer between audio and tajweed rule classification.

**Known limitations:** No published PER metric found. Training dataset size/diversity unknown. Consistent recitation style in training data (may lack learner-error diversity). No linked paper.

**HuggingFace:** https://huggingface.co/TBOGamer22/wav2vec2-quran-phonetics  
**Upload date:** December 9, 2025

**Sakīna action:** Test immediately — it's callable via HF Inference API. Use the `Buraaq/quran-audio-text-dataset` word-level clips as input and measure whether output phonemes match expected tajweed phonemes from `Hetchy/quranic-phonemizer`. If PER < 15% it becomes the candidate Sakīna phoneme layer (replacing the planned manual phonemizer + alignment approach).

## Finding 3 — Quran-MD (arXiv:2601.17880, Jan 25, 2026): Official Paper for `Buraaq/quran-audio-text-dataset` — 77K Word-Level Clips

**What it is:** Formal paper published January 25, 2026 for `Buraaq/quran-audio-text-dataset`. Authors: Muhammad Umar Salman, Mohammad Areeb Qazi, Mohammed Talha Alam.

**Stats:**
- **77,429 word-level audio samples** (each Quranic word as individual audio clip)
- **187,080 verse-level audio samples** (complete ayahs)
- **32 distinct reciters** (reflects diverse recitation styles)
- **Total size: 36.1 GB**
- Each sample: Arabic script + English translation + phonetic transliteration + aligned audio

**Why this matters vs. prior SUMMARY note:**
The SUMMARY mentioned `Buraaq/quran-audio-text-dataset` as "264,509 MP3s, 30 reciters, NeurIPS 2025" — this is now clarified: 77,429 word-level + 187,080 verse-level = ~264,509 total files, 32 reciters (not 30), with a formal January 2026 paper (not just NeurIPS 2025 poster).

**Key for Sakīna:** These 77,429 word-level clips are the ideal fine-tuning corpus for a phoneme-level tajweed model. Each word is isolated, labeled, and aligned — exactly the right granularity to train `TBOGamer22/wav2vec2-quran-phonetics` or an AraS2P variant to detect per-word tajweed violations.

**arXiv:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.17880  
**HuggingFace dataset:** https://huggingface.co/datasets/Buraaq/quran-audio-text-dataset  
**License:** Check paper (appears research-only)

## Finding 5 — Munsit Formal Paper (arXiv:2504.12254, Apr 2025): CNTXT AI's 15K-Hour Conformer — Technical Details Now Available

**What it is:** The technical paper behind CNTXT AI's Munsit ASR system. Already mentioned in SUMMARY as a product but now the underlying paper is confirmed with exact specs.

**Architecture specs:**
- **Model:** Large Conformer, 18 layers, ~**121M parameters**, trained FROM SCRATCH
- **Input:** 80-channel mel-spectrograms
- **Training data:** 30,000 hours of raw Arabic audio → filtered to **15,000 hours** high-quality via weakly supervised cross-model hypothesis filtering + language model plausibility scoring
- **Weak supervision method:** Multiple ASR models generate hypotheses → Levenshtein distance comparison selects consistent ones → LM grammar scoring → final pseudo-labels

**WER results (general Arabic, not Quran):**
- Munsit-1: WER **26.68**, CER **10.05**
- vs. best Whisper: WER 36.86 — **23% relative improvement**
- Munsit Edge (May 2026, on-device): WER ~**24%** across Gulf/Egyptian/Levantine/MSA/code-switch

**Critical gap for Sakīna:** WER 26.68 on general Arabic but NO Quran test. This is a dialectal/MSA model; Quranic Classical Arabic has different phonology. The FastConformer architecture used by Tarteel (nvidia/stt_ar_fastconformer_hybrid_large_pcd_v1.0) is likely superior for Quran because it was trained on Quranic data. Munsit Edge SDK remains worth evaluating for Sakīna's on-device path but needs Quran fine-tuning.

**arXiv:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.12254  
**IEEE:** https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/11166308/

## Synthesis: Sakīna Phoneme-to-Tajweed Stack (Updated After Round 13)

**Current state of the art for Sakīna's tajweed detection pipeline:**

```
[Mic audio]
    ↓
[ASR: KheemP/whisper-base-quran-lora OR Moonshine Arabic]  → Arabic text (WER ~5.63-5.98%)
    ↓
[Phoneme extraction: AraS2P (IqraEval 2025 #1) OR TBOGamer22/wav2vec2-quran-phonetics]  → phoneme sequence
    ↓
[Expected phonemes: Hetchy/quranic-phonemizer]  → expected phoneme + tajweed rules
    ↓
[Diff + classify: obadx/muaalem-model-v3_2]  → specific tajweed rule violated
    ↓
[User feedback: "You pronounced ن incorrectly — Ikhfaa rule at word 3"]
```
